Model Information

Historical MLB Dataset

Each model is trained on 3 past seasons of MLB data. The data consists primaily of moneyline, head-to-head pricing in the MLB market. It trains to identify high-quality price discrepancies -- differences between what the market thinks an outcome should be priced and the price at which your sportsbook is offering that outcome.

The dataset is first filtered for +EV bets. These by themselves are profitable in the long run -- it's like getting 3:1 on 'red' at the roulette table when the probability is just around 50%. The difference between SmartBetter and +EV betting sites is that our model trains to boost it's hit rate on +EV bets. Every time you win a +EV bet, you gain a winner and remove a loser, increasing money on money return dramatically.

Example

Lets look at an example: The LA Dodgers are one of the biggest market teams in the MLB. This implies that they have more fans to bet on their games. Therefore, to keep their books balanced, DraftKings might artificially increase the odds for their opponent. This reduces exposure. Our models identify this pattern and tend to bet more frequently on small-market teams when they play large-market teams because they will frequently be underpriced.
